Client Report

For my project, I decided to make a video game review website. I found pictures that matched the games I reviewed and wrote about some of the best single-player and multiplayer games.

I emailed my wireframe to my lead developer, and our communication went well. There weren't any issues.

The homepage content I asked for was added, but some parts weren't placed exactly how I pictured them in my wireframe. As of now, I haven't seen any updates on the child pages.

If I were to hire someone again, I'd want to make sure they have a good reputation by talking to people who have worked with them before.

Lead Developer Report

As a lead developer, I set up a Teams group chat for my team. I shared the wireframe that I was given and assigned each junior developer a child page to work on. I followed up each week to see how they were doing and asked if they needed anything from me.

Talmage did a great job creating the "Services" page. Mitchell hasn't made any progress on his "About" page so far.

I talk with Zachary in class, through email and Teams messages. I asked if he wanted to change anything on the homepage, and he said he liked how it looked.

I added the junior developers to Zachary's GitHub repo and sent him the link to his website.

This was my first time using GitHub. It was a bit confusing at first, but I got a lot of help from my teacher and classmates. I learned how to use it pretty quickly.

Junior Developer Report

As a junior developer, I used Teams to talk to my lead. Communication went smoothly, and I didn't have any big problems.

I worked on one child page for each of the two websites I was assigned to. For Brad Holbrook's project, I was given a wireframe for the painting page but no specific images. I picked one I thought worked and checked with my lead, who said it was fine.

For Ethan Shower's project, I wasn't given a wireframe or any details for the contact page. I made a simple form and asked if it was okay, and my lead said it looked good.

I didn't run into any issues with GitHub while working on these pages.

General Experience and Reflections

The most difficult part of this project was working with wireframes that were either incomplete or hard to follow. That made it tricky to know what the client really wanted.

Still, I really enjoyed making my own wireframe and learning how to use GitHub. I feel more confident now and know I'll be able to use it again in the future.

If I could do the project over, I would spend more time learning how to add cool hover effects and make the homepage more interesting.

This project helped me feel more ready for real-world work. I understand how GitHub works better now, and I learned how important clear communication is when you're working with a team—whether you're leading it or just helping out.
